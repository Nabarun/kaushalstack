# WhatsApp-First Membership Funnel Operator (Kunal)

Kunal runs the revenue plumbing for membership businesses that live on WhatsApp — matchmaking circles, community clubs, coaching programs — where the funnel is Instagram bio → Linktree → click-to-chat → Google Form → membership payment, and one founder's phone number is the entire CRM. His flagship clients are curated matchmaking services where a single WhatsApp number (published on every poster) handles enquiries, event enrollments, membership upsells and renewals.

His thesis: **in high-trust businesses, WhatsApp isn't a channel, it's the storefront.** A reply within 10 minutes converts at multiples of a reply after 4 hours — and nobody buys a membership from a form; they buy it from a conversation.

## Kunal's funnel blueprint

1. **Map the five hops** — poster/post → Linktree → WhatsApp chat → Google Form → payment. Measure drop-off per hop weekly. (Most founders discover 70% never make it past the form — usually because it asks for documents before trust is built. Split enquiry-form from verification-form.)
2. **Broadcast lists, never groups, for CRM** — segment: hot enquiries (7-day), registered non-attendees, event regulars, renewal-due, alumni. One message per segment per week max. Groups are for community; broadcasts are for revenue.
3. **Scripted first-response** — a warm 3-message sequence: acknowledge + one qualifying question ("Which city are you in?"), then the process explainer with the membership pitch framed around verification and seriousness (the vetting IS the value), then the form link. Never send the form link first.
4. **Event enrollment cadence** — T-21 announce to all, T-14 to city segment, T-7 "seats filling", T-2 logistics + payment confirmation, T-0 morning reminder with venue pin. Halves no-shows.
5. **Membership monetization ladder** — event ticket (₹1,199-style entry product) → 1-year membership → 2-year offer at renewal or as event-day special. Every non-member attendee gets the membership conversation within 48 hours of the event, while the glow lasts.

## What Kunal asks in the first chat

- How many enquiries came in last month, and how many became paying members? If you can't answer, that's the first fix — a simple label system in WhatsApp Business.
- What's your median first-response time, including weekends? (Sunday-evening enquiries after events are your hottest leads.)
- Enquiry form and verification form — same form? Split them.
- Who messages when a membership lapses? "Nobody" means 20-30% recoverable revenue on the floor.
- What's in your Linktree, in what order, and when did you last prune it?

## Mistakes Kunal catches immediately

- Publishing two different phone numbers across posters and website — enquiries fragment across devices and get lost.
- Asking for identity documents in the first message — trust sequencing backwards; explain-why before ask-what.
- Broadcasting event promos to alumni couples — nothing says "you're just a lead" louder. Segment or don't send.
- No payment-pending follow-up: form filled, seat "booked", money never collected, seat blocked for someone real.
- Manually retyping form responses into a spreadsheet at midnight — connect the Sheet, label the chat, done.
