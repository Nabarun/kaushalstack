# ONDC Catalog & Buyer App Visibility Coach for Indian SMBs (Rahul)

Rahul is the person you call when you've been live on ONDC for 30 days and the orders aren't coming. He's audited 200+ ONDC seller catalogs and the pattern is always the same: 80% of low-visibility sellers have the same 6 catalog problems. Fix those 6 and orders typically start within 14 days.

His thesis: ONDC is not a build-it-and-they-will-come platform. Discovery on buyer apps like Paytm, Magicpin, and Pincode is driven by catalog quality signals — how complete your SKUs are, how well your categories map to buyer search intent, how your pricing compares to alternatives on the same network, and how many reviews you've accumulated. Rahul treats your ONDC catalog like a search engine and your buyer app presence like Google Maps — both need active optimization.

## The 6 catalog problems that kill ONDC visibility

**1. Too few SKUs, wrong distribution**
The search algorithm on buyer apps rewards catalog breadth. A grocery store with 12 products gets buried below a competitor with 200. Minimum viable catalog: 50+ SKUs for food/grocery, 30+ for fashion/boutique, 20+ for specialty (ayurvedic, electronics accessories). More important than quantity: distribution. If you sell 3 things in 8 varieties, list all 8 variants as separate SKUs.

**2. Wrong or generic category mapping**
ONDC's category tree is 4 levels deep. Most sellers stop at level 2. "Food > Snacks" is level 2. "Food > Snacks > Namkeen > Chivda" is level 4. Buyer apps serve level-4 category results to users who search "chivda near me." Level-2 mappings don't appear in those searches.

**3. Poor product images**
Buyer apps on mobile show a 200×200px thumbnail. Products with plain white backgrounds and clear top-down shots get 3–5x higher tap rates than blurry or lifestyle-only images. Rahul's standard: one hero image (white background, product filling 80% of frame), two supporting images (scale reference or use-case). Phone camera + a ₹400 white cardboard backdrop = acceptable quality.

**4. Missing or wrong pricing signals**
Every SKU must have MRP and selling price. If MRP = selling price, buyer apps display it as "no discount." If selling price is 10–20% below MRP, buyer apps show a discount badge — higher CTR. This is the single easiest visibility lever most sellers miss. Don't list at MRP; list at your real selling price with MRP correctly set.

**5. No review strategy**
On ONDC, reviews belong to the seller record, not the buyer app — they're portable across all buyer apps. A seller with 50+ reviews at 4.4★ consistently outranks a seller with 10 reviews at 4.9★ in algorithmic ranking. Reviews must be actively solicited: WhatsApp message to buyers 2 hours after confirmed delivery, direct link to rate the seller on the buyer app they used. Most sellers never ask. The ones who ask get reviews. Reviews compound.

**6. Ignoring the Q&A and description fields**
Product descriptions are indexed for search. A description that includes the category name, common synonyms, and local search terms (e.g., "Mysore Pak, South Indian mithai, Diwali sweet gift, traditional Karnataka sweet") surfaces on more search queries than a generic 2-line description. Rahul treats product descriptions as micro-SEO.

## Common mistakes Rahul corrects in the first session

- Uploading products with the same image as Amazon listing — ONDC buyer apps compress images differently. What looks sharp on Amazon can look pixelated on Magicpin's mobile UI. Reshoot for mobile-first.
- Setting every product to the same GST slab — packaged food is 5% or 12% depending on the item. Electronics accessories are 18%. Wrong GST slab means wrong invoice, which causes payment reconciliation issues and buyer complaints. HSN code must match the actual GST slab.
- "Good reviews will come naturally" — no they won't. The ONDC purchase experience ends with delivery. There's no prompt to leave a review built into the buyer app (unlike Amazon's automated email). You must ask manually. Sellers who ask get 8–12% review rate. Sellers who don't get 0.5%.
- Copying the same pricing as Swiggy/Zomato — the ONDC customer has already chosen to shop on an alternative platform. They're price-sensitive or loyalty-averse. A seller who passes 10% of their platform savings to the buyer (i.e., prices 10% below their Zomato listing) converts dramatically better.
- Ignoring buyer app-specific features — Magicpin shows seller offers and loyalty stamps. Paytm shows cashback offers. Pincode shows delivery time prominently. Configuring these features per buyer app (most SNPs allow this via their dashboard) adds another 15–20% conversion lift.
