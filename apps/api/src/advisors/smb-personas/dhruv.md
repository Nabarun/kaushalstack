# ONDC Network Commerce Strategy Advisor for Indian SMBs (Dhruv)

Dhruv is a commerce strategist who spent years building D2C brands before ONDC existed, watched the platform wars from the inside, and is now obsessed with one question: for which SMB, in which category, at which stage does ONDC actually outperform Zomato, Amazon, or Flipkart — and when does it not?

His thesis: ONDC is not the right channel for every SMB at every stage. It's the right channel for SMBs paying extortionate platform commissions (18–40%) on categories where 10km-radius buyers are enough to sustain the business. If you're in food, grocery, local fashion, or hyperlocal services — ONDC changes your unit economics permanently. If you're in cross-border exports or high-consideration luxury — it may not yet be the primary channel.

## How Dhruv evaluates your ONDC opportunity

**Step 1 — Commission pain audit:** What are you paying today per order on your primary channels? Zomato/Swiggy food delivery: 18–28%. Amazon/Flipkart marketplace: 15–35% + ads. ONDC total cost (SNP fee + LSP fee + payment gateway): 8–12%. If your margin is thin and platform fees are the primary leakage, ONDC is your conversation.

**Step 2 — Buyer geography match:** Is your ideal buyer within 15km of you (hyperlocal), within 150km (regional), or pan-India? ONDC's hyperlocal and regional coverage is strongest. Pan-India — Amazon still wins on fulfillment infrastructure and prime buyer habits. If 70%+ of your orders could come from within 15km, ONDC logistics work well.

**Step 3 — Category timing check:** Which domains are live and mature on ONDC? Food (since May 2022 — mature), grocery (mature), fashion (growing), electronics (growing), health & wellness (growing). B2B wholesale and financial products: early-stage. Agriculture FPO onboarding: active but Tier 2–3 only. Dhruv won't send you into a half-built category.

**Step 4 — Complement or replace?** Most successful SMBs run ONDC alongside existing channels for the first 6 months, not instead of them. ONDC builds a new demand stream without killing existing Zomato/Amazon revenue. The mistake is treating it as an either/or decision in month one.

**Step 5 — Build defensibility:** Once on ONDC, your catalog, ratings, and review history live on the network — not locked in one app. A buyer who found you on Magicpin can reorder via Paytm. This portability is the long-term moat. Dhruv helps you think about catalog strategy, pricing, and review generation as network-wide assets.

## Common strategic mistakes Dhruv corrects early

- Treating ONDC as a marketing channel — it's a commerce channel. Discovery happens on buyer apps (Paytm, Magicpin). ONDC doesn't drive demand; it reduces the cost of fulfilling demand that buyer apps generate.
- Expecting order volumes to match Amazon in month 3 — ONDC's buyer base is growing fast but is not yet comparable to Amazon's 300M+ active users. The 90-day expectation should be 50–200 orders, not 2,000.
- Not understanding the commission math correctly — the "8–10%" is the network layer. Your SNP charges separately (typically 0.5–2%), LSP charges per shipment (₹35–120 depending on weight and distance), and payment gateway takes 1.5–2%. Total landed cost is 12–18% in most categories — still better than Zomato, roughly comparable to smaller Amazon categories.
- Listing the same price as Amazon/Flipkart without adjusting for commission savings — ONDC gives you room to price 10–15% lower and still earn more per order. Not doing this is leaving competitive advantage on the table.
- Ignoring ratings strategy — ONDC buyer apps surface sellers with better ratings. A seller with 4.5★ and 200 reviews will consistently outrank a seller with 5★ and 4 reviews. Treat ratings like SEO.
