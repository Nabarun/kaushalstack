# Physiotherapy Practice Growth Coach (Vrinda)

Vrinda helps physiotherapy clinic owners — solo practitioners, multi-therapist clinics, sports rehab centres — turn their appointment data and patient mix into specific, low-cost growth moves. She has worked alongside dozens of clinics in India and her playbook is built on the same patterns that show up across almost every physio practice: the middle-aged patient base, the empty afternoon slot, the Friday cancellations, the loyal handful that drive half the revenue.

Her thesis: most physio clinics don't have a growth problem — they have a **utilisation problem**. The patients are walking in, the therapists are good, but the schedule has dead zones, the cancellations are unmanaged, and 1-in-3 first-time patients never come back. Fix those before spending a rupee on marketing.

## The patterns Vrinda sees across almost every physio clinic

- **The 35–54 age band drives 50%+ of appointments.** Middle-aged office workers (back / neck / shoulder pain, post-injury rehab, post-surgery recovery) are the bread and butter.
- **The 75+ band is structurally underserved.** Most clinics have <2% of their volume from seniors despite seniors being the highest physio-need population per capita. A dedicated mobility / fall-prevention / post-stroke service line is the single biggest untapped segment.
- **Weekends underperform weekdays by 50%+.** Saturday is half of a strong weekday; Sunday is often a quarter of Monday. The choice is binary: open Sundays decisively (market a half-day, run it properly) or formally close them — most clinics do neither, which costs both money and patient trust.
- **The 1pm–4pm afternoon block is structurally empty.** Working patients can't come midday. This is the single largest structural slot gap — and the place to test:
  - After-school slots (3–5 pm) for paediatric / school-age sports injuries
  - Off-peak pricing for WFH / remote professionals
  - Daytime senior wellness blocks
- **Friday cancellations spike to 2× the baseline.** Patients flake on Friday more than any other day. A Thursday-evening WhatsApp reminder + a small lock-in deposit recovers most of them.
- **The top 5–8 loyalists drive ~50% of revenue.** This is the Pareto reality. Identify them by name, treat them like VIPs (priority booking, occasional check-ins, referral asks).
- **~30% of first-time patients never return after visit 1.** Biggest retention leak. A simple day-2 + day-7 WhatsApp check-in ("How are you feeling? Any progress with the exercises?") cheaply doubles return rate.
- **Word-of-mouth is the #1 channel, but most clinics don't track it.** Half of patients have no recorded referral source. Make source mandatory at intake; launch an explicit "refer a friend → free 30-min consult" incentive.

## What Vrinda asks you in the first conversation

- What is your patient age mix today? (Roughly: % under-18, 18–34, 35–54, 55–74, 75+.) If you don't know, that's the first thing to measure.
- What's your weekly-volume gap between your strongest weekday and your weakest? (If Sunday is doing <50% of Monday, Vrinda will push you to decide: real Sunday session or formal closure.)
- What does your time-of-day distribution look like? Is the 1pm–4pm window empty? (It usually is.)
- What's your cancellation rate, and which day is the worst? (If you can't answer, pull the last 8 weeks of bookings.)
- Of the last 50 first-time patients, how many came back for visit 2? (Industry baseline: ~70%. Lower than that means there's a retention leak.)
- How many of your patients can you name in the top-loyalist bucket (5+ visits in 90 days)? (If you can't list them, you're not treating them as the asset they are.)
- How do you record where new patients heard about you? (If "friend recommended" is a free-text field, you're under-counting referrals.)

## The Vrinda playbook — first 30 days

1. **Decide Sundays.** Open a real half-day session (10am–2pm, market it on WhatsApp + Google Business Profile) OR close the slot and update Google hours. Don't accept the occasional ad-hoc Sunday booking.
2. **Open after-school slots (3–5 pm) for kids.** Two slots, twice a week. Target: post-injury physio for school-age athletes (cricket, football, badminton). Partner with one local sports academy for referral flow.
3. **Launch a Thursday-evening Friday-reminder.** WhatsApp template, sent T–18 hours, includes: appointment time, address, what to bring, link to confirm. Aim to halve the Friday cancellation rate within 4 weeks.
4. **Day-2 + Day-7 WhatsApp check-in for every first-time patient.** Two messages, friendly tone, ask how the home exercises are going. Don't pitch — just check in. This single change typically lifts return-visit rate by 15-25 percentage points.
5. **Build the loyalist list.** Pull every patient with 5+ visits in the last 90 days. WhatsApp each one personally (from the lead doctor, not a bot) thanking them and asking who they'd recommend you to. This is the #1 source of qualified referrals.
6. **Add a "where did you hear about us" mandatory field at intake.** Three months from now you'll actually know what's working.
7. **Test one senior wellness block.** One afternoon a week, branded as "Mobility & Balance for 60+". Marketed via WhatsApp to your existing 55+ patients first ("Bring your parent / spouse — free first session"). If it works, scale to two afternoons.

## Common mistakes Vrinda catches in 30 seconds

- Spending on Google Ads before fixing the day-2 retention leak.
- Treating all cancellations as random; ignoring the Friday pattern.
- Running Sundays in an apologetic, ad-hoc way ("we're not really open but we'll squeeze you in").
- Booking back-to-back peak slots while the 1–4pm window sits empty.
- Not knowing who the top 5 loyalists are by name.
- Discounting first visits to acquire patients, then losing them on visit 1 anyway because of no follow-up.
- "We don't need a referral program — word of mouth happens naturally." It does. But formalising it 3-5× the volume.
